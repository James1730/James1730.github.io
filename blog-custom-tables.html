Custom Pool Tables for Hotels & Resorts: Design Options, Lead Times, and What to Expect

📅 March 15, 2026 • ✍️ Sundays Editorial • ⏱ 10 min read

A billiard table in a luxury hotel or resort isn't just recreation — it's a design statement. When guests walk into a game room and see a beautifully crafted table that matches the interior aesthetic, it elevates the entire experience. But how do you get a custom table that meets both design standards and professional playing quality?

As a manufacturer that has produced custom tables for hotels, resorts, and private clubs across 50+ countries, we'll walk you through the entire process — from initial concept to delivery.

What Can Be Customized?

Almost everything. Here's a comprehensive breakdown of customization options:

1. Wood Finish & Color

  • Standard finishes: Natural oak, walnut, mahogany, ebony black, pure white
  • RAL color matching: Send us any RAL code and we'll match it precisely. Popular hotel choices include RAL 9010 (pure white), RAL 7016 (anthracite grey), and RAL 8017 (chocolate brown)
  • Special techniques: High-gloss piano lacquer, distressed vintage, hand-rubbed oil finish, metallic paint

2. Leg Design

  • Classic turned legs: Traditional billiard hall aesthetic with carved details
  • Modern tapered legs: Clean lines for contemporary interiors
  • Industrial steel frame: Powder-coated steel with exposed hardware
  • Pedestal base: Minimalist two-pedestal design for ultra-modern spaces
  • Custom carved: Hand-carved patterns, animal motifs, or architectural details

3. Cloth Color & Material

  • Standard 12 colors in stock (green, blue, red, burgundy, grey, black, etc.)
  • Custom Pantone matching: Any color to match your brand or interior scheme
  • Branded cloth: Your logo printed directly on the playing surface (using heat transfer technology that doesn't affect ball roll)
  • Material upgrades: Tournament-grade 100% worsted wool for professional play

4. Branding & Logo

  • Hot foil stamping: Gold, silver, or color foil on rails or frame (most popular)
  • Laser engraving: Permanent engraving on wooden surfaces
  • Metal badge: Custom die-cast metal logo plate on the rail
  • Rail inlay: Mother-of-pearl or metal inlay work for ultra-premium projects

5. Multi-Function Options

  • Dining top conversion: Fitted hardwood or glass dining surface that sits on top of the playing surface
  • Conference table mode: Integrated power outlets and cable management for corporate settings
  • Ping pong conversion: Tournament-standard table tennis top included

Pricing: What Drives the Cost?

Custom tables cost 15–40% more than standard models, depending on the complexity. Here are the main cost factors:

FactorCost ImpactNotes
RAL color matching+5–10%Requires custom paint mixing and testing
Custom leg design+10–25%New mold or hand-carving labor
Logo engraving+$20–50/tableOne-time setup fee for laser program
Branded cloth+$30–80/tableHeat transfer printing
Dining top conversion+$150–400/tableDepends on material (wood vs. tempered glass)
Premium slate upgrade+$100–200/tableThicker or imported slate
Tip: Ordering 10+ tables of the same custom design significantly reduces per-unit costs. The setup fees (molds, paint mixing, logo programming) are amortized across the batch.
