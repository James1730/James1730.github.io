2026 Ultimate Guide for Importing Billiard Tables from China

📅 July 05, 2026 • ✍️ Supply Chain Editorial • ⏱ 15 min read

Importing high-value, heavy items like billiard tables requires careful planning. With slate beds weighing over 400kg, logistics and customs clearance are the biggest challenges for international buyers. This guide covers everything you need to know for a smooth import process in 2026.

1. Understanding HS Codes for Billiard Tables

The standard HS Code for billiard tables and accessories is 9504.20. Knowing this code is crucial for calculating import duties and taxes in your destination country. Whether you're in the USA, Europe, or the Middle East, this code identifies your shipment as gaming equipment.

2. Shipping: LCL vs. FCL

Because of the weight and fragility of the slate, we highly recommend FCL (Full Container Load) if you are ordering more than 3 tables. FCL reduces handling at transit ports, significantly lowering the risk of damage. For single-table orders, LCL (Less than Container Load) is possible, but requires extra-reinforced wooden crating.

3. Required Documentation for Customs

To clear customs efficiently, you'll need three main documents from us: the Commercial Invoice, Packing List, and Bill of Lading (B/L). For certain markets, a Certificate of Origin (CO) or CE certification for electronic components (like LED lights) may also be required.
