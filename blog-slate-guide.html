The Ultimate Guide: How to Choose Professional Slate for Snooker and Pool Tables

📅 July 01, 2026 • ✍️ Technical Editorial • ⏱ 12 min read

The slate bed is the heart of any professional billiard table. It provides the perfectly flat surface required for true ball rolling. But not all slate is created equal. At Sundays Billiard, we prioritize Grade-A slate to ensure tournament-level performance.

1. Why Slate Thickness Matters

For professional snooker tables, 45mm to 50mm thickness is the standard. Thicker slate provides better stability and absorbs vibrations, ensuring that the ball rebound is consistent across the entire table. For home pool tables, 25mm (1 inch) is the minimum required for a quality game.

2. CNC Milling and Precision

Modern manufacturing uses CNC (Computer Numerical Control) milling to process slate. At Sundays, our slate is milled with a tolerance under 0.1mm. This extreme precision ensures that the three or five pieces of slate fit together seamlessly, creating a unified playing surface that remains level for decades.

3. The "Natural vs. Synthetic" Debate

Natural slate, specifically from regions like Jiujiang in China, is world-renowned for its fine grain and durability. Unlike synthetic alternatives, natural slate doesn't warp or expand with humidity changes, making it the only choice for competition-grade tables.
